HIPAA COMPLIANCE RULES

================================================

// All personnel are legally bound by the following regulations. Select each rule to expand.

Covered entities may only use or disclose Protected Health Information (PHI) as permitted or required by the Privacy Rule. PHI includes any individually identifiable health information held or transmitted in any form.

  • PHI must not be disclosed without written patient authorisation except in specific permitted cases.
  • Minimum Necessary Standard applies: disclose only what is required for the stated purpose.
  • Patients have the right to access, correct, and restrict use of their own health information.

All electronic Protected Health Information (ePHI) must be secured with technical safeguards to control access, prevent unauthorised access, and ensure data integrity.

  • Implement role-based access controls. Only authorised personnel may access ePHI.
  • All ePHI must be encrypted at rest and in transit using industry-standard protocols.
  • Automatic logoff must be enabled on all systems that handle ePHI.
  • Audit logs must record all access to and modifications of ePHI systems.

In the event of a breach of unsecured PHI, covered entities must notify affected individuals, the Secretary of HHS, and in some cases, the media.

  • Affected individuals must be notified without unreasonable delay and within 60 calendar days of discovery.
  • HHS must be notified. Breaches affecting 500+ individuals require immediate notification.
  • Notifications must state: the nature of the breach, data types involved, and steps individuals should take.

Covered entities must implement administrative policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations.

  • All workforce members must receive HIPAA training upon hire and periodically thereafter.
  • A designated Privacy Officer and Security Officer must be assigned.
  • Sanctions must be applied to workforce members who violate HIPAA policies.
  • A contingency plan must exist for emergencies affecting systems containing ePHI.

Physical access to systems containing ePHI must be controlled and monitored. Workstations and devices must be protected from unauthorised access.

  • Facility access controls must limit physical access to authorised individuals only.
  • Workstation use policies must specify proper usage of all systems that access ePHI.
  • Device and media controls must govern the movement and disposal of hardware containing ePHI.

When using or disclosing PHI, you must make reasonable efforts to limit information to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose.

  • Do not access patient records beyond what your role requires. Curiosity is not authorisation.
  • Routine PHI requests must be limited by policies identifying what is needed for each use.
  • This standard does not apply to disclosures to the patient or those required by law.

HHS enforces HIPAA rules and may investigate complaints and conduct compliance reviews. Civil and criminal penalties apply to violations.

  • Tier 1 — Unknown violation: $100–$50,000 per violation, max $25,000/year per category.
  • Tier 2 — Reasonable cause: $1,000–$50,000 per violation, max $100,000/year.
  • Tier 3–4 — Wilful neglect: $10,000–$50,000+ per violation, max $1.9M/year.
  • Criminal penalties can include up to 10 years imprisonment for knowing misuse of PHI.
